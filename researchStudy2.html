Interaction Design in VR

Testing Three VR Apps

Summary

VR Experience Testing is a research project that seeks to test different modes of interaction in a 3D environment. The Gear VR was used with three apps and a diverse participant group. Virtual reality is still in it's infancy stages and understanding how users respond to different modes of interaction can assist future design and development in VR applications.

Tasks & Responsibilities

  • Facilitating usability tests, statistical analysis.
  • Qualitative data collection

Questionnaires

Simulator Sickness Questionnaire

Why test this?

Exposure to virtual reality can cause symptoms similar to motion sickness. I wanted to see if there was a relationship between interaction type and symptoms of simulation sickness.

Results

1. Simulator Sickness Questionnaire

  • #1 Least reports of sickness - WSJ Video (camera controlled by head movements).
  • #2 Ocean Rift (user controls movement with Gear VR)
  • #3 Most reports of sickness - Land’s End (movement controlled by fixating on objectives).

2. System Usability Scale Questionaire

  • #1 - Highest averaged SUS scores 80% - WSJ Video (camera controlled by head movements).
  • #2 - 71% Land’s End(movement controlled by fixating on objectives).
  • #3 - Lowest averaged SUS scores 62% - Ocean Rift (user controls movement with Gear VR controls).

3. Enjoyability (user preference)

  • Winner (Overall Favorite) - WSJ Video (camera controlled by head movements).
  • Second place is a two way tie between Lands End and Ocean Rift. Overall users had ranked both apps ‘favorite’, ‘2nd favorite’ and ‘least favorite equally’.

Take Aways

  • My findings indicate that users prefer spectating with camera control over the interactions that require user movement & objection completion.
  • I received IRB approval for virtual reality testing on potentially sensitive populations.
  • Facilitated usability tests while only able to verbally explain tasks and processes to users who were effectively blind to me because of the headset.
  • Anticipate resistance to new technology in elderly populations (generally speaking)
